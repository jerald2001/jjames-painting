## 1. Executive Summary

J. James Painting Contractors is a family-owned Sunshine Coast painting business established in **1985** (formerly Hume and Staff Painters) with over **30 years** of trade experience across residential, commercial, and industrial work. The existing website is a functional but generic Elementor build that reads as a broad "we do everything" painting contractor — it does not currently capture the four high-margin specialty offerings the business wants to grow.

The redesign has two jobs:

1. **Reposition** the brand around four signature service lines that suit the Sunshine Coast property market — investor repaints, pre-sale prep, new-home buyer refreshes, and boutique residential work.
2. **Convert better** — turn anonymous local search traffic into booked free quotes through clearer messaging, stronger trust signals, real project photography, and a frictionless quote flow.

The site should still serve commercial/industrial inquiries (these are bread-and-butter), but the homepage and primary navigation should lead with the four growth services.

## 4. Target Audiences

### Audience A — Rental property owners & property managers
- Own one or more investment properties on the Sunshine Coast.
- Need fast turnaround between tenancies; minimum disruption.
- Care about durable, neutral finishes that suit market expectations.
- Decision drivers: speed, price predictability, ability to coordinate with PMs and tenants, depreciation-friendly invoicing.

### Audience B — Vendors preparing to sell
- Selling a home in the next 30–90 days.
- Often working with a real estate agent who recommended a refresh.
- Need a high-impact, cost-controlled paint job that lifts sale price.
- Decision drivers: speed, presentation quality, working around inspections and staging.

### Audience C — New home buyers
- Recently purchased a home, planning to move in.
- Want to repaint before furniture arrives — empty house, easier job, fresh start.
- Decision drivers: timeline (settlement to move-in), colour consultation, the chance to "make it theirs."

### Audience D — Boutique homeowners
- Owners of higher-end Sunshine Coast homes (Noosa, Sunshine Beach, Peregian, Buderim hinterland, Maleny).
- Architectural, character, or coastal premium properties.
- Value craftsmanship, finish quality, and the painter's eye for detail.
- Decision drivers: portfolio quality, colour expertise, respect for the property, attention to detail.

### Audience E — Existing referral channels (secondary but important)
- Real estate agents, property managers, builders, body corporates, conveyancers.
- Need a reliable, communicative, insured contractor they can recommend without risk.

## 6. The Four Priority Service Lines (Deep Dive)

These four services are the strategic focus of the redesign. Each needs its **own dedicated landing page**, its own positioning, its own visual treatment, and its own conversion path. They are not just listed alongside the legacy services — they lead.

### 6.1 Rental Property Repaint

**Who it's for:** Investors and property managers between tenancies, or refreshing tired rentals to lift rental yield.

**Page content blocks needed:**
- Hero: "Repaint between tenants. Re-let faster." (or similar)
- Why landlords choose J. James: speed, durability, neutral palettes that appeal to tenants, minimal disruption.
- The repaint-between-tenants process: walkthrough → quote → schedule → painted in X days → ready for inspection.
- Durable finishes that survive tenants (semi-gloss trims, washable wall paints, scuff-resistant systems).
- Working with property managers: direct invoicing, coordination, key handover, before/after photos for owner reports.
- Tax & depreciation note (general, not advice): repainting is a deductible repair in many cases — talk to your accountant.
- FAQ: turnaround times, working around tenants, packages for multi-property portfolios.
- CTA: "Get a rental repaint quote" form (with property address, # bedrooms, occupied/vacant, target start date).

**Target keywords:** rental property painter Sunshine Coast, investment property repaint, between tenancy painting, landlord painter Noosa/Caloundra/etc.

### 6.2 House Pre-Sale Painting

**Who it's for:** Vendors and their real estate agents preparing a home for market.

**Page content blocks needed:**
- Hero: "Painted to sell. Ready for inspection in days, not weeks."
- The ROI angle: a fresh coat is one of the highest-return pre-sale improvements.
- Agent-recommended palettes: neutral, market-ready colours that photograph well and broaden buyer appeal.
- Tight pre-listing timelines: we can quote, schedule, and complete inside [X-day] windows.
- Coordination with stylists, stagers, photographers, and agents.
- Before/after case studies (these must be real — see Content Requirements).
- Specific scopes: full repaint, exterior only, "freshen up" packages (touch-ups + high-traffic walls + trims).
- Real estate agent partner CTA: "Are you an agent? Become a referral partner."
- Vendor CTA: "Get a pre-sale quote" form (address, list date, scope wanted).

**Target keywords:** pre-sale house painter Sunshine Coast, painting to sell my house, vendor paint refresh, real estate painter Noosa.

### 6.3 New Home Purchase Painting

**Who it's for:** Buyers who've just settled and want to repaint before moving in.

**Page content blocks needed:**
- Hero: "Just bought a place? Paint it before you move in."
- The why: empty house = faster, cheaper, cleaner job; no furniture to shift; no living in dust.
- Settlement-to-keys timeline: how to coordinate with your conveyancer and removalist.
- Colour consultation included — "make it feel like yours from day one."
- Whole-home packages with transparent pricing tiers (e.g. interior walls only / walls + trims + ceilings / full repaint inside and out).
- Working with builders' final defect lists if it's a brand-new build.
- Conveyancer / buyer's agent referral angle.
- CTA: "Get a new-home quote" form (settlement date, move-in date, address).

**Target keywords:** new home painter Sunshine Coast, repaint before moving in, settlement painting, house painter for new owners.

### 6.4 Boutique House Painting

**Who it's for:** Owners of architecturally significant, coastal premium, character, or high-end homes who want craftsmanship-level work.

**Page content blocks needed:**
- Hero: large hero image of a genuinely premium project. Less copy, more visual confidence.
- Positioning: "Painting for homes that deserve more than a quick coat."
- The difference: extra prep, expert surface diagnosis, premium product systems, master-painter-level finish, colour consultation.
- Specialisms: heritage and Queenslander homes, architectural new builds, feature walls and bespoke finishes, limewash and specialty coatings, high ceilings and difficult access.
- Process: site visit and consultation → detailed proposal → product and colour recommendations → meticulous prep → finish → final walkthrough.
- Project gallery — this section earns the page (see Content Requirements).
- Suburb relevance: Noosa, Sunshine Beach, Peregian, Maleny, Buderim hinterland.
- CTA: "Book a consultation" (positioned as a conversation, not a quick quote).

**Target keywords:** boutique house painter Sunshine Coast, premium house painter Noosa, architectural painter, high-end residential painter QLD.

## 10. Design Direction

### Visual identity
- **Refresh, don't replace** the existing logo (keep brand recognition built up since 1985) unless the client wants a full rebrand.
- New colour palette suggestions: a clean, confident core palette — deep coastal navy or charcoal as the primary, warm off-white as the background, a single accent colour for CTAs (a confident terracotta or muted teal sits well with the Sunshine Coast aesthetic). Final palette to be confirmed with the client.
- Typography: a clear, modern sans-serif for body, paired with a slightly editorial serif or a confident display sans for headlines. Avoid the default Elementor look.

### Photography
- **The single biggest visual upgrade.** The current site relies on generic stock-feeling imagery. A photography day on three or four real Sunshine Coast projects (ideally one for each of the four service lines) will transform the site.
- Drone shots of exterior work are particularly strong for boutique and pre-sale pages.

### Layout & UX principles
- Generous whitespace, large imagery, short body copy in scannable blocks.
- Sticky header with phone number and "Get a Quote" button always visible.
- Mobile-first — most quote requests will come from phones.
- Page load under 2.5s on 4G.

## 15. Tech Stack Recommendation

Two viable paths:

### Option A — Stay on WordPress (recommended if budget is tight)
- Keep WordPress but **replace Elementor** with a faster, cleaner builder (Bricks or a custom theme).
- Hosting: a managed WP host (Kinsta, WP Engine, or local equivalent) for speed.
- Plugins: Yoast or Rank Math (SEO), WP Rocket (caching), Fluent Forms or Gravity Forms (quote form), Smush (images).
- Pros: lower cost, existing team familiarity, easy editing.
- Cons: still a CMS — needs maintenance.

### Option B — Modern Jamstack / headless (recommended if budget allows)
- Framework: Next.js / Astro static site, content via a headless CMS (Sanity, Storyblok, or Payload).
- Hosting: Vercel / Netlify.
- Pros: blazing fast, excellent SEO, secure, low maintenance.
- Cons: higher build cost, more technical to update for non-devs.

**Recommendation:** Option A unless the client wants to invest in a longer-term, higher-performance platform.
